Indian Mathematicians and Their Contributions

Aryabhata (476 CE – 550 CE)

  • Mathematics: Introduced zero as a place value, solved linear equations, and calculated areas and volumes.
  • Trigonometry: Defined sine functions and created accurate sine tables.
  • Astronomy: Explained Earth's rotation, calculated the solar year length, and predicted eclipses.

Brahmagupta (598 CE – 668 CE)

  • Zero: Treated zero as a number and defined its arithmetic operations.
  • Algebra: Solved quadratic equations and introduced negative numbers.
  • Geometry: Developed Brahmagupta’s formula for cyclic quadrilaterals and methods for approximating square roots.
  • Astronomy: Studied planetary motion and calculated Earth’s circumference.

Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887 CE – 1920 CE)

  • Number Theory: Developed Ramanujan primes and the partition function.
  • Infinite Series: Created series for Pi and approximations for logarithmic functions.
  • Hardy-Ramanujan Number: Discovered 1729, the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two ways.
  • Elliptic Functions: Made advances in modular forms and mathematical analysis.
